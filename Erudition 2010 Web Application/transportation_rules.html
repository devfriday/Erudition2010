Rules :


 

Overview:


1.    The goal of this event is to encourage the students to take part in industrial projects.
2.    Participants should propose a practical and economically viable solution.
3.    The winners from the first round will present their ideas at GGITM.

Instructions to the participants


1.    Identify and propose a solution with details for validation.
2.    Problem Solution should be supported by established facts.
3.    Participants can refer VETAS website for the wind turbine auxiliaries’ details.

Objective :


To study the current transportation modes & conditions and arrive at the most efficient and safe transport concept Factors to be considered are:
1.    Cost
2.    On-time Delivery
3.    Quality:
4.    Corrosion
5.    Vibrations
6.    Dirt
7.    Forced entry
8.    Material damages
9.    Students are expected explore the possible modes and come up with innovative & realistic ideas.
10.  All students who register for the event will be given further information regarding product specifications and other relevant details.

Evaluation Criteria                                                 Weightage (%) 
Data collection relevant to the transportation        20 
cost of the solution                                                 20 
Viability of the solution                                           20 
Details of modes of transportation                         20 
Team strength and commitment                            20
Rounds

 

 

Fantastic Eight


  Fifteen teams will be selected after Online screening of all the entire out of this eight teams will be selected for the next round.

Tricky Three


Selected eight teams should present their plan at GGITM in form of a PowerPoint presentation.

How to submit your idea

  • 1. Indicate your interest by sending an email to  Dhrone09@yahoo.com with your team details.
  • 2. Submit a complete proposal with supporting facts.
  • 3. Benefits of the solution.
  • 4. Cost of the solution with supporting documents.
  • 5. Timeline.
  • 6. How will you measure success.

    • Team Details :


    1. Brief 1 page resumes of each team member.
  • 2. Participating team should comprise of 2 members.
  • 3. If your proposal is selected in the second round, you need to attend ERUDITION and present your solution.

Guidelines 

  • 1. For Industrial problem solving, you may form groups of max 3 persons.

  • 2. A team has to prepare an succinct abstract indicating:

    • a) Overview of the problem at hand
    • b) Factors to be considered in design
    • c) Brief overview of the design / design alternatives (if available)
    • d) Advantages of the proposed design Versus the available alternatives

  • 3. The abstract should include a cover page indicating the following:

    • Names of participants:
    • Email id :
    • College / University name :
    • Full postal address of the college:
    • One contact number :

 

  • 4. Please give the email ids which you will check regularly. The email id will be the primary source of communication between GGITM and you from the time of your submission till the event completes.

  • 5. The abstract has to be a maximum of two pages of A4 size stationary (excluding the cover page). The team has to submit a soft copy of the abstract with the above sections, typed out in Times New Roman (Size 12) with single line spacing.

  • 6. The abstracts not in the above formats will be summarily rejected.

  • 7. The team has to send the abstract as an attachment on or before 11th of april to the mail intimating their submission to Dhrone09@gmail.com

  • 8. Entries received after this date will not be considered.

  • 9. The short listed teams will have to demonstrate the complete solution at Erudition. The duration for each team is 20 minutes followed by 10 minutes of Q&A session.

  • 10. Only three winner  industrial problem will be chosen and awarded. The decision of the judges is final and binding on all participants.
